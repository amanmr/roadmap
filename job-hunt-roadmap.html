🧠 Daily DSA Approach (FAANG Level)

Pattern first, solution second. Before writing code, identify the pattern: sliding window, two pointers, BFS/DFS, DP, heap, trie. Name it out loud. FAANG interviewers reward pattern recognition over memorized solutions.

Talk while you code. Narrate your thinking. Silence kills you in FAANG interviews. Say "I'm thinking about edge cases" or "I'll start with a brute force then optimize."

Time yourself. Easy: 10 min. Medium: 25 min. Hard: 45 min. If you exceed it, note why — don't just watch solutions.

🏗️ System Design Framework (use every time)

1. Requirements (5 min) — Clarify functional + non-functional. Ask: scale, latency, consistency requirements.

2. Capacity Estimation (3 min) — RPS, storage, bandwidth. Show you can do back-of-envelope math.

3. High-Level Design (10 min) — Draw boxes: client, LB, API servers, DB, cache, CDN, queue.

4. Deep Dive (15 min) — Interviewer will pick 1-2 components. Go deep on DB schema, cache strategy, consistency model, failure handling.

5. Trade-offs (5 min) — What would you do differently at 10× scale? What did you sacrifice?

🎯 Project — Your Secret Weapon

Your distributed cache project covers: LRU, TTL, WAL, LSM Tree, Bloom Filters, Consistent Hashing, Gossip Protocol, Read Repair, Raft Consensus, Micrometer metrics. That's 10 distributed systems concepts in one project.

When asked "design a distributed cache" — you don't design it. You built it. Walk them through your actual implementation. This is a rare and powerful position. Use it.

Target companies where this lands hardest: Any company with a distributed systems / backend / platform engineering team — Amazon, Google, Uber, LinkedIn, Razorpay, PhonePe, Flipkart.
